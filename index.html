nafSadh

Sadh MNM Khan
My preferred name is 'Sadh'. I'm also known as 'Khan M Nafee Mostafa' on documents and as 'nafSadh' online.
I am a software engineer with interest in linguistics, epistemology, logic, natural language understanding, large language models, and AI/ML. I have over a decade of experience in the industry and have built a wide range of systems and infrastructures from the ground up, with a focus on backend development.

I enjoy reading books, indulging in my love for tea, going on hikes, biking and writing. I co-founded Lekhalikli.com, an online Bengali literary publishing platform that aimed to nurture amateur writers and contribute to the growth of Bengali literature. Although the platform is now defunct, I still have an interest in literature and writing. Read my blog, notes, Bengali literary works and answers.

I studied Computer Science in Stony Brook (MS) and KUET (BS). I grew up in Bangladesh and have lived in New York and SF Bay Area. I am always looking for new challenges and opportunities to apply my skills and knowledge in the field of large language models and generative AI.

work and projects

Currently, I am working at Google on a team that explores the possibilities of large language models and AI. Before this, I worked on Android App Safety, where I led multiple cross-org collaborations to build and maintain the infrastructure behind Android's trust and safety ecosystem. I also built Google Nest's backend ML platform that powers Home/Away Presence Sensing and Home Routines.

Before Google, I built TCI's Loan Origination SaaS and in my first job at Samsung, I worked on custom system software, DSP, and SoC.

I am also involved in a number of open source projects, including Flags.fyi, a repository of flags for vexillophiles, and Sonnivo, a Bengali keyboard.

Visit my résumé (ré.sadh.mé), LinkedIn and GitHub for more info.
